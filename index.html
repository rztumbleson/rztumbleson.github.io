About Me

Hi, Zoey here. I am a sixth year physics PhD student at the University of California, Santa Cruz. I work in experimental condensed matter physics and primarily study magnetic spin textures and their thermodynamic phase transitions. I specialize in the technique of coherent X-ray scattering and am based at the Advanced Light Source (ALS) at Berkeley Lab. I am a frequent user of synchrotron and X-ray free electron laser facilities (XFEL) to study ultra-fast dynamics of magnetic systems and am particularly interested in the intersection of data science and X-ray science to discover novel phases of matter.
