About Me

Hi, Zoey here. I recently earned my PhD in physics from the University of California, Santa Cruz. As an experimental condensed matter physicist, my focus was on understanding phase transitions of different types of order in materials using resonant soft X-ray scattering with an emphasis on magnetic spin textures. I specialized in the technique of coherent X-ray scattering and was based at the Advanced Light Source (ALS) at Berkeley Lab. I am a frequent user of synchrotron and X-ray free electron laser facilities (XFEL) to study ultra-fast dynamics of magnetic systems and am particularly interested in the intersection of data science and X-ray science to discover novel phases of matter. Check out my recent publications on our discovery of magnetic nematic phases in Science Advances. Alternatively check out one of these general audience science highlights written by SLAC or the Advanced Light Source at LBNL.
